The History of Disney

The Walt Disney Company was first created in the 1920s by Walt Disney. The year 1928 marks the year that Steamboat Willie was released and it was the first animated film with sound that featured the famous character, Mickey Mouse. The very first Disney feature-length animated film was "Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs" which premiered in 1937. During World War II, Walt Disney's movies didn't do as good as they used to. However, in the 1950s, things started to look a little brighter for Disney. The Disneyland theme park first opened on July 17, 1955. Disneyland is now one of the most poplular theme parks in the world with a total of six parks all across the globe, welcoming millions of visitors through their gates. Unfortunately, Walt Disney passed away on December 15, 1966. Disney may be almost a century old but you'll never be too old to enjoy Disney.

Pixar

"Mnemonic Device" by Clint from Flickr.com CC BY 2.0

Pixar is an animation studio that was first created in 1979. There headquarters is located in Emeryville, California. In 1991, Pixar and Disney make their first movie together, and that movie is known as "Toy Story". Toy Story is now one of the most well-known Disney Pixar movies made. They've made more aninmated movies such as "Toy Story 2" and 3, "Up", "Monster's, Inc", "The Incredibles", "Cars" and "Finding Nemo". The Walt Disney Company agreed to buy Pixar Studios in the year of 2006, which was 14 years ago. Two of their most recent movies have gone on to make history. The first was "The Incredibles 2" which grossed $1.2 billion worldwide and that's Pixar's highest-grossing film to date. Their second movie, "Toy Story 4" won an academy award for 'Best Animated Feature' at the 92nd annual Academy Awards.

Marvel

"Marvel Logo" by Anthony Yanez from creativecommons.org CC BY 4.0

Marvel is a very famous brand and they first started in 1939 with the release of the first Timely Comics issue, named "Marvel Comics No.1". After World War II, however, the people started to lose interest in the Marvel superheroes. In the 1960s, Stan Lee caused a revival of the Marvel superheroes. Marvel Studios was only bought by Disney in 2009, which has only been 11 years, which really isn't a lot. Some of the Marvel movies that have been produced since 2008 with Kevin Fiege as President of Marvel Studios include "Iron Man", "Captain America", "Thor", "Avengers", "Black Panther", "Doctor Strange", and "Spiderman: Homecoming". Their last Marvel movie "Avengers: Endgame" broke box-office records and now holds the record for highest-grossing film earning $2.79 billion worldwide. (Fun Fact: Have you ever wondered why X-Men and the Avengers never met each other on screen before, even though they're both under the Marvel brand? Well the answer is because during the 1990s, Marvel sold the rights to a lot of their characters).

Lucasfilm

Star Wars

"Star Wars" by Andrés Felipe Guevara Rincón is licensed under CC BY-NC-ND 4.0

The film production studio Lucasfilm was founded in 1971 by the filmmaker George Lucas. One of their most famous motion picture is "Star Wars". The first "Star Wars" movie came out in 1977. Disney bought recently bought Lucasfilm in 2012, only 8 years ago. There are a total of nine Star Wars movies with many spin-offs including "Solo" "Rogue One", and the animated series "Clash of the Clones". The last Star Wars movie to hit theaters was "Star Wars: The Rise of Skywalker" in 2019 and it grossed upwards a total of $1 billion.
